Use this style when converting Chinese literature-reading Markdown reports into lab-meeting decks.

Do not modify the reference deck. Do not copy it as a template unless the user explicitly asks. Use it to infer a restrained academic journal-club style.

Observed style cues:

- 16:9 academic presentation format.
- Blue, white, black, gray, and red emphasis.
- Common colors: navy `#194A96`, white `#FFFFFF`, black `#000000`, gray `#7F7F7F`, red `#D43E43` or `#FF0000`, pale blue `#9CC5FD`.
- Chinese font direction: Microsoft YaHei or PingFang SC.
- English font direction: Times New Roman or another readable serif for paper titles and figure labels.
- Main content is figure-led, with short explanatory Chinese labels.
- Red is used sparingly for emphasis, arrows, highlights, or key conclusions.

## Layout Principles

- Keep a consistent title position and visual rhythm.
- Use large paper figure modules as the dominant evidence.
- Prefer concise claim titles instead of section labels.
- Use short callouts near the relevant part of the figure.
- Keep generous margins around dense scientific figures.
- Use light backgrounds for most content slides.
- Use a darker or more typographic cover and final summary only when it improves hierarchy.

## Slide Patterns

### Cover

- Paper title dominates.
- Add journal, year, presenter, and a short Chinese framing sentence.
- Keep it cleaner than a marketing cover.

### Background / Question

- Use 2-4 short statements or a simple causal diagram.
- Avoid dense paragraph blocks.

### Study Design

- Use an AI method schematic if available.
- Pair cohort/sample details with one compact table or timeline.

### Result Slide

Recommended composition:

- top: conclusion-style title
- center or right: large figure module
- side or bottom: 2-3 concise interpretation bullets
- optional: red outline, arrow, or label highlighting the key region

### Summary

- Use 3-5 numbered takeaways.
- If a final mechanism AI schematic exists, place it large and annotate with short labels.

## Avoid

- Text-only slides.
- Whole-page figure screenshots.
- Long Markdown paragraphs copied into slides.
- Tiny unreadable figure crops.
- Repeated card grids.
- Overuse of red.
- Decorative animations.
- Generic corporate pitch-deck layouts.
